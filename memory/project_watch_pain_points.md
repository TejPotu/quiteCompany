Pain points collected from real patients that the Watch tab must address:

1. AI handles platform logistics — patients shouldn't see/choose platforms
2. Patients forget which platform has which show
3. Same show on multiple platforms causes episode/season confusion
4. Patients can't get out of a show or switch safely
5. Cursor/focus/selection are hard to track visually
6. Rewinding behaves differently per platform
7. Patients fear shows will disappear forever when finished
8. Anxiety causes premature restarting of series
9. Small text, jargon, missing captions increase confusion

**Why:** these came directly from observed/interviewed patients, not designer guesses. They are the source-of-truth requirements list for the Watch tab and any related calling/watching surfaces.

**How to apply:** for any Watch-tab change, check it against these nine items. Specifically: never show platform names to the user; never list a show twice; always provide a plain-language "Stop watching" exit; make focus/selection redundantly obvious (outline + badge + dim non-selected); use human time for rewind ("a little", "a lot"); always reassure that shows are kept and place is saved; show resume points so users don't restart from scratch; keep text large and captions visible by default.
